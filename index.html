What Is Mardi Gras?

Mardi Gras is an annual festival held in New Orleans, Louisiana, which takes place in the weeks leading up to and including Fat Tuesday, which is the day before Ash Wednesday. The festival is known for its elaborate parades, colorful costumes, and lively celebrations. The first Mardi Gras parade in New Orleans was held in 1837, and the tradition has continued ever since, with various krewes (social organizations) creating and sponsoring their own parades and events throughout the city. The largest and most famous parades are held on the last five days leading up to Fat Tuesday, with the final parade being the "Zulu" parade, followed by the "Rex" parade. Read more
